Children's Life Insurance Canada: Should You Get It? A Parent's Guide

As parents, we want to protect our children from every possible harm. But when it comes to children's life insurance in Canada, many families wonder: do we really need it? It's a question I hear often at WealthTalk with Ekbir, and the answer might surprise you.

Life insurance for children isn't about preparing for a tragedy—it's about smart financial planning. Let me walk you through what you need to know.

What Is Children's Life Insurance in Canada?

Children's life insurance is a policy that provides a death benefit if your child passes away. In Canada, these policies typically offer coverage amounts ranging from $5,000 to $25,000, though some insurers allow higher limits.

There are two main types:

  • Term life insurance for children: Covers a specific period (10, 20, or 30 years) at a lower, fixed premium
  • Permanent life insurance for children: Covers your child for their entire life and includes a cash value component

The premiums are remarkably affordable because children are young and statistically have very low mortality rates. At WealthTalk with Ekbir, I've helped many Canadian families secure coverage for less than $15-20 per month.

Why Consider Children's Life Insurance?

The primary reason isn't morbid—it's practical. Here are the genuine benefits:

1. Final Expense Coverage

Funeral and burial costs in Canada average $7,000-$12,000. If tragedy strikes, life insurance ensures you're not financially devastated while grieving. This is especially important for immigrant families who may want to arrange services according to their cultural and religious practices.

2. Locking in Insurability

One of the smartest reasons to get children's life insurance early: you're securing coverage while your child is healthy. If your child develops a health condition later in life, they may face higher premiums or rejection when they apply as an adult. By getting coverage now, they're guaranteed insurability.

3. Building Cash Value (if you choose permanent insurance)

Permanent policies accumulate cash value over time. Your child can borrow against this, use it for education costs, or tap it for life events when they're older.

4. Peace of Mind

For many families, especially in the South Asian community where children are deeply valued, knowing you're protected provides invaluable emotional security.

Do You Actually Need Children's Life Insurance?

This depends on your situation. You should seriously consider it if:

  • Your family depends on a child's income (older teens working)
  • Your child has special healthcare needs or medical history
  • You want to guarantee their insurability as an adult
  • You have outstanding debts and want to protect your family's financial stability
  • You're planning to leave an inheritance or education fund
  • You want a forced savings vehicle with permanent insurance

You might skip it if:

  • You already have adequate life insurance as the primary earner
  • Your emergency fund can cover funeral costs
  • Your family financial situation is extremely tight

What Canadian Parents Should Know

Canada has excellent healthcare, but that doesn't eliminate all risks. Accidents, rare illnesses, and unforeseen circumstances do happen. The Canada Safety Council reports that unintentional injuries are the leading cause of death for Canadian children aged 1-14.

Additionally, if you're a newcomer to Canada or sponsoring family members under Super Visa or spousal sponsorship, life insurance—including coverage for dependents—should be part of your overall financial plan.

Here's what I always tell families: children's life insurance is one of the most affordable insurance products available. The cost-benefit ratio is excellent. You're spending roughly $150-240 per year to eliminate a potential $10,000+ financial crisis.
