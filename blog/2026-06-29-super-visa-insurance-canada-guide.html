Super Visa Insurance Canada: Complete Guide for Parents & Grandparents

If you're bringing your parents or grandparents to Canada for an extended visit, you've likely heard about Super Visa insurance. This critical requirement isn't just a formality—it's a mandatory insurance policy that protects your family and ensures Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) approves your Super Visa application.

What Is Super Visa Insurance Canada?

Super Visa insurance is a private health insurance policy specifically designed for parents and grandparents visiting Canada for extended periods. Unlike standard travel insurance, it's mandatory for Super Visa applications and must meet strict IRCC requirements.

The Super Visa program allows eligible parents and grandparents to stay in Canada for up to two years per visit without renewing their visa. However, to qualify, visitors must have valid health insurance coverage from day one of their arrival.

This insurance covers medical emergencies while your parents or grandparents are in Canada—protecting them, your family, and Canadian healthcare resources. The policy must be obtained before arrival and cannot be purchased after entering Canada.

Super Visa Insurance Coverage Requirements

IRCC has specific requirements for Super Visa insurance policies. Understanding these ensures your application won't be rejected due to inadequate coverage.

Minimum Coverage Amount

Your Super Visa insurance must provide a minimum of $100,000 CAD in medical coverage. This covers emergency medical expenses, hospital stays, and urgent procedures. Many families opt for higher coverage—$150,000 to $200,000—for additional peace of mind.

Duration Requirements

The insurance policy must cover the entire duration of the Super Visa stay. Since Super Visas allow up to two-year stays, your insurance should cover the full period, though annual renewal is common.

Pre-existing Conditions

Most Super Visa insurance policies cover pre-existing medical conditions without waiting periods—a crucial benefit for older parents dealing with diabetes, hypertension, or heart conditions. However, some carriers may apply limitations, so it's essential to review your specific policy.

Required Coverage Components

Valid Super Visa insurance must include:

  • Emergency medical care and hospitalization
  • Emergency dental treatment (up to specified limits)
  • Emergency medical evacuation and repatriation
  • Coverage across all Canadian provinces and territories
  • No exclusions for age (though premiums increase with age)

Super Visa Insurance Costs in Canada

Super Visa insurance costs vary based on several factors. Understanding pricing helps you budget and select appropriate coverage.

For a single parent aged 60-65, annual Super Visa insurance typically costs between $600–$900 CAD. For visitors aged 66-70, expect $800–$1,200 CAD annually. Applicants over 70 may pay $1,200–$2,000+ CAD depending on health status and chosen coverage limits.

Several factors influence your premium:

  • Age: The primary cost driver; older visitors pay significantly more
  • Health Status: Pre-existing conditions may increase premiums slightly
  • Coverage Amount: Higher limits ($150,000–$200,000) cost more than minimum coverage
  • Duration: Longer stays cost more; two-year policies exceed annual rates
  • Insurance Provider: Different carriers offer varying rates and benefits
