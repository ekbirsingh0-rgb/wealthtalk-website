What Are Group Benefits and Why Do Small Businesses Need Them?

Group benefits are insurance and wellness programs that employers offer to their employees as part of their compensation package. These typically include:

  • Extended health coverage (prescriptions, dental, vision, therapy)
  • Life insurance
  • Disability insurance (short-term and long-term)
  • Wellness programs and mental health support
  • Accidental death and dismemberment (AD&D) coverage

In Manitoba, small businesses with just 5-10 employees often wonder if group benefits are worth the investment. The answer is absolutely yes. Here's why:

Employee Retention: Employees who have access to health benefits stay with their employers longer. In competitive job markets like Winnipeg's, this gives you an edge.

Tax Advantages: In Canada, employer contributions to group benefits are tax-deductible, which means you save money on your business taxes.

Employee Productivity: When your team knows they're covered for medical expenses, mental health support, and prescription costs, they're healthier and more focused on work.

Affordable Premiums: Because you're insuring a group, the cost per employee is much lower than individual policies. Group rates in Manitoba are competitive, especially when you work with an experienced broker.

Group Benefits Options for Manitoba Small Businesses

Not all small businesses are the same, and neither should their benefits packages. Here are common options:

Fully Insured Plans

With a fully insured plan, an insurance company manages everything—claims, administration, and coverage. This is ideal for businesses with 5-50 employees because it's simple and predictable. Your monthly premium stays the same regardless of claims.

Self-Insured Plans (Larger Groups)

If you have 50+ employees, you might consider self-insuring some coverage. This means you retain some risk but can save money if your group has fewer claims than expected.

Flexible Benefit Plans (Cafeteria Plans)

Let your employees choose benefits that suit their life stage. A young, single employee might prefer more dental coverage, while a parent might prioritize mental health and prescription benefits. This approach increases satisfaction and engagement.

What Does Group Benefits Cost in Winnipeg?

Cost is often the first question small business owners ask. In Manitoba, group benefits premiums vary based on several factors:

  • Number of employees (larger groups often get better rates)
  • Age profile of your workforce
  • Coverage levels you choose (basic vs. comprehensive)
  • Industry and claims history
  • Whether you include dental and vision coverage

For a small business in Winnipeg with 10 employees, you might expect to pay anywhere from $100-250 per employee per month for a solid package. Employers typically contribute 50-80% of the premium, with employees covering the rest through payroll deductions.

The good news? The tax deductions for your business often offset a significant portion of what you're paying.
