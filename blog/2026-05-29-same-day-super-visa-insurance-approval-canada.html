What Is Super Visa Insurance and Why Do You Need It?

Super Visa insurance is a mandatory requirement set by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) for parents and grandparents applying for a Super Visa. This temporary resident visa allows your parents to stay in Canada for up to two years without renewing their status.

Here's what makes it unique:

  • Mandatory requirement: Without valid Super Visa insurance, your parent's application will be rejected
  • Minimum coverage: $100,000 CAD for medical expenses and hospitalization
  • Medical exams required: Your parents will undergo a medical exam as part of the application process
  • Age consideration: Older applicants (typically 65+) often face higher premiums but can still get approved

The insurance must be purchased from a Canadian insurance company and must cover emergency medical care, hospitalization, and dental care (up to $200). It's designed to protect both your family and Canada's healthcare system.

What Affects Your Super Visa Insurance Approval Timeline

While same-day approval is standard for most applicants, a few factors can influence the timeline:

Health History: If your parent has pre-existing conditions like diabetes, hypertension, or heart disease, the insurance company may request additional medical information. This typically still resolves within 24-48 hours.

Age of Applicant: Parents aged 65 and older are generally more straightforward for approval, as age-appropriate premiums are already built into the system. Parents over 75 may require additional underwriting, but this rarely delays approval beyond one business day.

Completeness of Application: The faster you provide all requested information, the faster approval happens. Incomplete applications are the #1 reason for delays.

Time of Day Applied: Applications submitted before 2 PM EST typically see same-day approval. Applications submitted after hours are usually approved the next business day.
