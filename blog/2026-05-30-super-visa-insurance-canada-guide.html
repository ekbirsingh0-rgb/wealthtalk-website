Super Visa Insurance Canada: Complete Guide for Sponsoring Parents & Grandparents

If you're planning to sponsor your parents or grandparents to visit Canada, you've likely heard about Super Visa insurance. This mandatory requirement has helped thousands of South Asian Canadian families welcome their loved ones for extended stays. But what exactly is Super Visa insurance, and how do you navigate the process? Let's break it down together.

What Is Super Visa Insurance Canada?

Super Visa insurance is a specialized health insurance policy required by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) when you sponsor your parents or grandparents for an extended visit. Unlike regular travel insurance, Super Visa coverage is specifically designed to meet Canadian immigration requirements and provides comprehensive health protection during their stay in Canada.

The Super Visa program allows your parents and grandparents to stay in Canada for up to two years without renewing their visitor status. It's a wonderful way to spend meaningful time with family, whether they're visiting for a few months or planning a longer stay.

However, the IRCC requires proof of insurance before approving a Super Visa application. This isn't just a suggestion—it's a mandatory condition of the visa itself.

Super Visa Insurance Requirements: What IRCC Demands

To be approved for a Super Visa, your parents or grandparents must have Super Visa insurance that meets specific federal requirements. Here's what IRCC looks for:

  • Minimum coverage of $200,000 CAD: The policy must cover emergency medical care, including hospitalization and emergency dental work (up to $200).
  • Deductible not exceeding $2,500 CAD: This ensures the visitor shares some responsibility for healthcare costs, but the deductible remains reasonable.
  • Coverage for the entire stay in Canada: Insurance must be valid for the full duration of their visit, from arrival to departure.
  • Pre-existing conditions: Many approved policies cover pre-existing medical conditions (though some exclusions may apply).
  • Purchase before arrival: Insurance must be purchased before your family member arrives in Canada.

These requirements exist to ensure that Canadian healthcare systems aren't burdened with unexpected emergency costs, and to protect your visiting family members from devastating medical expenses.

How Much Does Super Visa Insurance Cost?

One of the most common questions we hear at WealthTalk with Ekbir is: "How much will this cost?" The answer depends on several factors.

Cost-determining factors include:

  • Age of the visitor (premiums increase significantly after age 65)
  • Duration of coverage needed (3 months vs. 2 years)
  • Existing health conditions
  • Insurance provider and policy type
  • Whether pre-existing conditions are covered

For example, a 70-year-old visiting for 3 months might pay between $400-$800 CAD, while a 2-year policy for the same person could range from $2,000-$4,500 CAD. A younger, healthier 55-year-old might secure similar coverage at significantly lower rates.

While cost matters, remember that this insurance protects your family from financial catastrophe. A single hospital visit in Canada can cost tens of thousands of dollars, so proper coverage is an investment in peace of mind.
