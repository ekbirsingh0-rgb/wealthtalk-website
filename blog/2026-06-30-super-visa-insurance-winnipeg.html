What is Super Visa Insurance and Why Do You Need It?

The Super Visa is a special Canadian immigration program that allows parents and grandparents of Canadian citizens or permanent residents to visit Canada for extended periods—up to two years per entry. It's designed to keep families together while maintaining Canada's immigration integrity.

Super Visa insurance is mandatory. You cannot even apply for a Super Visa without proof of private health insurance coverage. This insurance must meet specific Government of Canada requirements and provide a minimum of $100,000 CAD in health coverage.

Here's why this matters: your parents may face unexpected medical emergencies during their visit. Without proper insurance, a single hospitalization could cost tens of thousands of dollars. Hospital stays, medications, emergency dental work, and specialist consultations can quickly drain savings. Super Visa insurance protects both your family's finances and your parents' wellbeing.

Super Visa Insurance Requirements in Canada

The Government of Canada has strict requirements for Super Visa insurance policies. Understanding these helps you avoid application rejection and ensures your parents have adequate coverage.

Minimum Coverage Amount

Your insurance policy must provide at least $100,000 CAD in medical coverage. This covers hospital fees, doctor visits, prescription medications, and emergency dental treatment.

Coverage Duration

Your policy must be valid for the entire duration of your parents' stay in Canada. Most families purchase annual policies, which can be renewed if their visit extends beyond one year.

Additional Requirements

  • Policy must be purchased from a Canadian insurance company or international provider approved by Canada
  • Insurance must cover the applicant's entire stay in Canada
  • No waiting periods exceeding 30 days for pre-existing conditions
  • Coverage must include emergency medical evacuation
  • Policy must be in effect before your parents arrive in Canada

What Super Visa Insurance Covers

It's essential to understand what your Super Visa insurance actually covers. Most policies include:

  • Hospital services: Room, meals, nursing care, and operating room costs
  • Doctor consultations: Both emergency and non-emergency visits
  • Prescription medications: Drugs prescribed during treatment
  • Emergency dental: Treatment for pain relief (cosmetic work usually excluded)
  • Ambulance services: Ground and air ambulance costs
  • Medical evacuation: Emergency transport to your home country if needed
  • Lab tests and X-rays: Diagnostic imaging and blood work

However, pre-existing conditions may have waiting periods, and routine check-ups or preventative care might not be fully covered. This is why choosing the right policy matters.

Cost and Coverage Considerations

Super Visa insurance typically costs between $2,000 and $4,500 CAD annually for seniors aged 65 and older, depending on age and health status. Younger parents pay less. While this might seem significant, it's a small investment compared to potential medical costs.

Factors affecting your premium include your parents' age, health conditions, coverage amount, and deductible chosen. Higher deductibles mean lower premiums but higher out-of-pocket costs if they need care.
