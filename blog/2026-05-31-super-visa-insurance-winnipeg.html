What Exactly Is Super Visa Insurance?

Super Visa insurance is specialized medical coverage designed specifically for parents and grandparents visiting Canada on a Super Visa. Unlike regular travel insurance, Super Visa insurance is built to meet the strict requirements set by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC).

The policy must provide a minimum of $100,000 in hospital and medical coverage. It must also have a maximum deductible of $500 per claim and must cover the entire duration of the Super Visa stay, which can be up to two years per entry.

What makes Super Visa insurance different from regular travel insurance is its flexibility with pre-existing medical conditions. Most travel insurance plans exclude or heavily restrict coverage for conditions your parent already has. Super Visa insurance, by contrast, is designed with the understanding that visitors in this age group will have existing health issues.

Super Visa Insurance Coverage: What's Included

A comprehensive Super Visa insurance policy typically covers:

  • Emergency medical expenses: Hospital stays, emergency room visits, and urgent care treatment
  • Doctor visits and consultations: General practitioners and specialist appointments
  • Prescription medications: Essential drugs prescribed during the visit
  • Dental emergencies: Pain relief and emergency extractions (though not routine cleanings)
  • Vision care: Emergency eye care and some optical services
  • Ambulance services: Ground ambulance transport to medical facilities
  • Medical evacuation: Air ambulance if your parent needs transport to a specialized facility

Cost and Duration of Super Visa Insurance in Winnipeg

The cost of Super Visa insurance depends on several factors: your parent's age, their health status, how long they're staying, and the coverage limits you choose.

For a parent aged 65-70 staying three months, you can expect to pay between $500-$1,200. For extended stays of 12-24 months, annual costs typically range from $1,500-$3,500. While this might seem like an expense, it's relatively affordable compared to the cost of even a single hospitalization in Canada, which can easily exceed $10,000.
