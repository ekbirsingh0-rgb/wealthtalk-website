What Is Super Visa Insurance and Why Do You Need It?

Super visa insurance is mandatory coverage for parents and grandparents visiting Canada under the Super Visa program. Unlike a standard visitor visa, the Super Visa allows parents to stay in Canada for up to two years at a time, making it the preferred option for many South Asian families.

The Government of Canada requires applicants to prove they have private health insurance that covers:

  • Hospital stays and emergency medical care
  • Doctor visits and prescription medications
  • Emergency dental and vision care
  • Medical evacuation if needed

The policy must provide minimum coverage of CAD $200,000 and be valid for at least one year. Without this insurance, your parents' Super Visa application will be rejected, regardless of their financial situation or health status.

Understanding Super Visa Insurance Monthly Payment Plans

Monthly payment plans have revolutionized how families afford super visa insurance. Rather than paying the full annual premium upfront—which can range from CAD $1,200 to CAD $3,000 depending on age and coverage—families can now spread costs across 12 months or even longer.

Here's how it typically works:

  • Annual Premium: The total cost calculated based on your parent's age, pre-existing conditions, and desired coverage level
  • Monthly Installments: Divided into equal payments, usually starting at CAD $100–$250 per month depending on the policy
  • Zero Interest Options: Many brokers offer interest-free installment plans, making the actual cost identical to paying upfront
  • Flexible Terms: You can often choose payment schedules that align with your pay cycle

How to Choose the Right Monthly Payment Plan

When evaluating super visa insurance monthly payment options, consider:

  • Your parent's age: Premiums increase with age, affecting monthly costs
  • Pre-existing conditions: Certain health conditions may increase or require special coverage
  • Payment flexibility: Does the plan allow you to pay bi-weekly, monthly, or quarterly?
  • No interest options: Ensure you're not paying more overall by choosing installments
  • Broker reputation: Work with licensed professionals who can answer questions in your language
