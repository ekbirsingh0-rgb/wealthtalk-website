What Is Super Visa Insurance and Why Do You Need It?

Super Visa insurance is mandatory medical coverage required by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) when your parents or grandparents apply for a Super Visa. This special visa category allows parents and grandparents to stay in Canada for up to two years per visit, making it ideal for extended family time.

The Super Visa insurance requirement exists to protect both your visiting family members and the Canadian healthcare system. It ensures that any medical emergencies during their stay are covered without burdening public healthcare resources. Think of it as peace of mind for your family during what should be a joyful visit.

In Calgary specifically, Super Visa insurance is not optional—it's a legal requirement for the visa application to be approved. Without valid coverage, your parents' application will be rejected, regardless of other qualifications.

Super Visa Insurance Coverage Requirements in Alberta

The Canadian government has specific minimum requirements for Super Visa insurance coverage that all policies must meet:

  • Minimum coverage amount: $100,000 CAD for emergency medical care
  • Duration: Coverage must extend for the entire duration of the Super Visa (up to 24 months)
  • Provider requirements: Insurance must come from a Canadian insurance company or provider approved by IRCC
  • Emergency services included: Hospital stays, emergency dental (up to $200), emergency vision care, and emergency prescription medications
  • Coverage start date: Must begin on or before the date your parent arrives in Canada

Many families don't realize that regular travel insurance won't work for Super Visa purposes. You need a policy specifically designed to meet IRCC requirements. That's where working with an experienced broker like myself becomes invaluable.

How Much Does Super Visa Insurance Cost in Calgary?

The cost of Super Visa insurance in Calgary depends on several factors, but families typically invest between $600 to $2,500 CAD annually for comprehensive coverage. Here's what affects pricing:

  • Your parent's age (most policies are available up to age 80-85)
  • Their pre-existing medical conditions
  • Length of stay in Canada
  • The insurance provider chosen
  • Whether coverage is for one person or multiple family members

While cost matters, remember that this insurance protects your family from potentially devastating medical bills. A single hospitalization in Calgary without coverage could cost $5,000 to $50,000 or more. The insurance premium is a small investment in your peace of mind.
