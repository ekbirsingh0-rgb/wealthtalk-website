What Is Super Visa Insurance & Why Is It Required?

The Super Visa is a multiple-entry visa that allows parents and grandparents of Canadian citizens and permanent residents to stay in Canada for up to 24 months per visit. Unlike other visitor visas, the Super Visa offers extended stay periods—perfect for families who want meaningful time together without the hassle of frequent visa renewals.

However, to qualify for a Super Visa, Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) mandates that your visiting parent or grandparent must have valid Super Visa insurance coverage from the moment they arrive until their departure. This isn't optional—it's a hard requirement, and applications without proof of insurance will be rejected.

The insurance must provide a minimum of $100,000 CAD in emergency medical coverage and be issued by a Canadian insurance provider licensed by the IRCC.

What Does Super Visa Insurance Cover?

Super Visa insurance typically covers:

  • Emergency medical care: Hospital visits, emergency dental treatment (up to $200), and emergency vision care
  • Prescription medications: Drugs prescribed by a Canadian physician
  • Medical transportation: Ambulance services and repatriation to your home country if needed
  • Pre-existing condition coverage: Unlike many travel insurance plans, Super Visa insurance can cover medical conditions that existed before the policy was issued (with proper declaration)
  • 24/7 multilingual support: Most providers offer customer service in English, Punjabi, Hindi, and other languages

What's not covered includes routine check-ups, dental cleaning, vision exams, or elective procedures. This is emergency medical coverage only—think of it as a safety net for unexpected health events.

Super Visa Insurance Costs for Brampton & Mississauga Residents

The cost of Super Visa insurance varies based on your parent's or grandparent's age, health status, and the length of their stay. Here's what you typically expect:

  • Age 65-74: $800–$1,500 CAD for one year of coverage
  • Age 75+: $1,500–$3,000 CAD for one year of coverage
  • Multi-year policies: Some insurers offer discounts for 2–5 year policies, bringing annual costs down by 10–20%

Monthly payment plans are available from most providers, making it easier for families in Brampton and Mississauga to budget. A parent visiting for six months might cost between $400–$800, depending on their age and health profile.

While these costs might seem high, they're an absolute bargain compared to what a single hospitalization in Canada could cost without coverage. A three-day hospital stay can easily exceed $5,000.
