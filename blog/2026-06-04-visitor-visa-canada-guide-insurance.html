What Is a Visitor Visa?

A visitor visa, officially called a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV), is a travel document that allows foreign nationals to enter Canada temporarily for tourism, business, or visiting family. It's not a work or study permit—it's specifically for people who want to spend time in Canada without intending to settle permanently.

For citizens of certain countries—including India, Pakistan, Philippines, China, and many others—a visitor visa is mandatory before boarding a plane to Canada. Even if you hold a valid passport, you cannot enter Canada without this visa if your country requires one.

The visa is typically valid for up to 10 years (for some nationalities), allowing multiple entries into Canada. Each visit can last up to six months, though immigration officers determine the actual duration upon arrival.

Key Requirements for a Visitor Visa Application

Successfully obtaining a visitor visa requires careful preparation. Here are the essential documents and requirements:

  • Valid Passport: Must be valid for the entire duration of your stay in Canada
  • Completed Application Form: IMM 5257 for most applicants
  • Proof of Financial Support: Bank statements, property deeds, or employment letters showing you can afford your visit
  • Travel Plans: Flight bookings, accommodation details, and itinerary
  • Ties to Your Home Country: Evidence that you'll return (property ownership, employment, family)
  • Medical Exam: Sometimes required for health screening
  • Police Clearance: Proof that you have no criminal record
  • Invitation Letter: If visiting family, a letter from your Canadian relative explaining the relationship and your visit purpose
  • Proof of Travel Insurance: Increasingly important for visa approval

The processing time varies, but applications typically take 2-4 weeks for most countries. Some applications are processed faster through expedited services.

Why Visitor Travel Insurance Is Critical

Many people overlook travel insurance when applying for a visitor visa, but it's one of the smartest investments you can make. Here's why:

Visa Approval: Immigration officers often view travel insurance as proof that you're a responsible traveler. It demonstrates you've planned carefully and can handle unexpected situations.

Medical Coverage in Canada: If you're from outside Canada, you're not covered by provincial health insurance. A hospital visit for an emergency can cost thousands of dollars. Travel insurance protects you from devastating medical bills. For example, an emergency room visit in Canada can cost $500-$2,000, and hospitalization can exceed $20,000 per day.

Trip Cancellation Protection: If a family emergency forces you to cancel your trip, travel insurance reimburses non-refundable flights and accommodations.

Lost Baggage and Delays: Your luggage contains important documents and valuables. Insurance covers losses.

Emergency Evacuation: If you become seriously ill and need to return home for treatment, travel insurance covers evacuation costs—sometimes exceeding $100,000.

Choosing the Right Visitor Insurance Plan

When selecting visitor insurance, look for plans that cover:

  • Emergency medical expenses (minimum $100,000 recommended)
  • Hospital and physician visits
  • Prescription medications
  • Dental emergencies
  • Trip cancellation and interruption
  • Lost or delayed baggage
  • Emergency medical evacuation

The cost varies based on your age, health status, and coverage level. Generally, a comprehensive 30-day visitor insurance plan for someone aged 30-40 costs between $60-$150, while coverage for seniors (65+) may range from $200-$500 for the same period.

If you're planning multiple visits to Canada over a year, an annual multi-trip visitor insurance plan offers better value than purchasing coverage for each trip separately.
