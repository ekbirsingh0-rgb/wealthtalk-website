What Is Super Visa Insurance and Why Do Your Parents Need It?

Super Visa insurance is a mandatory medical coverage requirement for parents and grandparents coming to Canada under the Super Visa program. This is not optional—without it, Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) will not approve your parents' visa application.

The Super Visa allows your parents to stay in Canada for up to two years per entry, making it perfect for extended family visits. However, Canadian healthcare doesn't automatically cover international visitors, so insurance protects both your family and your parents' health during their stay.

The policy must cover:

  • Emergency medical expenses up to a minimum of $100,000 CAD
  • Hospital visits and emergency dental work
  • Medical evacuation back to India if needed
  • Coverage for pre-existing medical conditions (with proper declaration)

This safety net gives your parents peace of mind and protects you from unexpected medical costs that could reach tens of thousands of dollars.

Super Visa Insurance Requirements for Brampton and Mississauga Families

IRCC has specific requirements that insurance policies must meet:

  • Minimum coverage: $100,000 CAD (some insurers offer up to $200,000)
  • Validity: The policy must be valid for the entire duration of your parents' stay in Canada
  • Medical exam: Your parents may need to undergo a medical assessment, depending on age and health status
  • Insurance provider: Only approved Canadian insurance companies can issue Super Visa policies
  • Pre-existing conditions: These can be covered if disclosed at the time of application

For families in Brampton and Mississauga with parents over 60 years old, premium costs typically range from $2,000 to $4,000 CAD for a one-year policy. Younger parents (under 60) usually pay $1,500 to $2,500 annually.

Choosing the Right Super Visa Insurance Provider

Not all insurance companies are created equal when it comes to Super Visa coverage. Here's what to look for:

  • IRCC-approved status: Verify the insurer is on the official approved list
  • Pre-existing condition coverage: Ensure your parents' health concerns are protected
  • Extended stay flexibility: Some policies allow stays up to 730 days (two years)
  • Transparent pricing: No hidden fees or surprise exclusions
  • Multilingual support: Customer service in Punjabi, Hindi, or English for peace of mind
